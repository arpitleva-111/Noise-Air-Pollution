Air Pollution

  • Particulate Matter (PM2.5): Fine particles from combustion, industrial processes, and vehicles.
  • Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2): Primarily from vehicle emissions and power plants.
  • Ozone (O3): Formed when pollutants react in sunlight, harmful at ground level.
  • Sulfur Dioxide (SO2): From burning fossil fuels, especially coal.

Noise Pollution

  • Transportation Noise: Vehicles, aircraft, and railways.
  • Industrial Noise: Factories, construction sites.
  • Residential Noise: Loud music, domestic activities, machines.
  • Occupational Noise: Workplace machinery and tools.

Both air and noise pollution stem from various human activities, largely concentrated in urban and industrial areas, making them complex challenges to address.

Measuring the Invisible: Tools and Metrics

Air Quality Sensors

Measure particulate matter (PM), ozone, NO2, SO2 and other gases using monitoring stations and portable devices.

Sound Level Meters

Quantify noise levels in decibels (dB) to assess compliance with regulatory thresholds and health guidelines.

Laboratory Analysis

Air and water samples undergo chemical analysis to identify specific pollutants and their concentrations.

Accurate measurement is the first step toward understanding the scale of the problem and evaluating mitigation efforts.
